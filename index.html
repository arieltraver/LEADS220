Mapshots

Geolocating the memories of Wellesley College

Introduction

How can people cherish the special moments in their lives and on campus? Students want to remember positive experiences they had in college, in order to feel more connected to the community. They want to interact with social media in a way that is less performative and more genuine.

  • Practice gratitude
  • Connect with the community
  • Share ideas with friends
  • Find special locations
  • Preserve Wellesley traditions
  • Take part in campus history

To address this problem we decided to design an app where students post experiences on a shared map. The map should show the collective memories of the student body and allow you to geolocate your experiences. The map should allow students to feel like they are taking part in campus history, and can help preserve campus traditions and old locations which might otherwise be lost due to time.

HTA 1

Emily is grateful for her big/little family at Wellesley. She wishes their future generations could see photos from their time here. She posts them on her Instagram and Facebook accounts, but she knows they will eventually get buried by other posts, and her littles' littles probably won’t see them. With our app, she can place the picture on the campus map. As future family members come along, they can continue the tradition and add photographs at the same location.

Task Tree 2

Emily Johnson is hoping to find a quiet place on campus to spend time with friends. First, she scrolls through her friends’ posts on Instagram, looking for images with nice locations. However, she gets distracted by advertisements and memes between all the posts, and loses some time. She then goes onto Twitter and makes a post asking for suggestions. However, she is deterred by the negativity and clickbait-style content which appears in her feed. Also, since her post isn’t interesting or inflammatory, it doesn’t get many views or responses.

Ideation & Testing

Imagine you are a Wellesley student who downloaded this app to document your fondest memories on campus with other students, faculty and alumni. You have some photos of when you met your best friend, Daisy, at Lake Waban. Daisy is the one who introduced you to this social platform and has sent a message on this app. After signing up, Daisy urges you to check what they sent you.

  1. Find a specific message
  2. View tags on posts
  3. Make and pin post to location
  • Count the number of clicks
  • Count the number of errors
  • Record time elapsed
Testing Feedback

Users reported that the message button should be more visible, the map should be movable and scroll. They mentioned that the camera button was misleading in its placement. They also said the tagging user feature was separated from pinning posts. Overall, they agreed the posting process should be more intuitive. This influenced our final prototype design.
